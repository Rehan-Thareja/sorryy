Dear Friend,

I owe you a sincere and heartfelt apology. When you told me about not feeling well, I didn't take you seriously. I mistakenly thought you were just making an excuse to skip school, and I even joked about it. That was thoughtless and inconsiderate of me.

I now understand that you had an asthma attack - something serious that should never be dismissed or taken lightly. Asthma attacks can be frightening and dangerous, and instead of supporting you during a difficult time, I added to your distress with my insensitivity.

I created this website not just as a creative way to say sorry, but to show you how much our friendship means to me and how deeply I regret hurting you. My words and actions were wrong, and you deserve better from a friend.

I hope you're feeling better now, and I want you to know that I'm here for you - to listen, to support, and to be the kind of friend you deserve.

"True friendship isn't about being there when it's convenient; it's about being there when it's not."

I promise to be more understanding, more supportive, and a better listener from now on.

From your friend who is truly sorry and cares about you.
